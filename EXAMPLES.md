## Quiz Mode

### English Grammar
Teach Grade 4 students about English articles (a, an, the). Use fill-in-the-blank questions with simple sentences they would understand from daily life.

Teach the difference between "a" and "an" with words starting with vowel and consonant sounds. Include tricky cases like "an hour" and "a university".

Teach Grade 5 students about prepositions (in, on, at, under, over). Each question is a fill-in-the-blank sentence about a child's school day.

Quiz on past tense verbs for Grade 3. Show simple sentences with the verb missing — students choose the correct past tense form.

Teach common English idioms for Grade 6 students. Show the sentence with the idiom blanked out, and let students pick the right idiom.

Subject-verb agreement quiz for Grade 4. Sentences where students choose between "is/are" or "has/have" or "was/were".

### Math
Multiplication tables practice for Grade 3 students. 5 questions covering tables 2 to 9. Make it fun and encouraging.

Fractions quiz for Grade 4 students. Show simple word problems and let them pick the correct fraction.

Word problems on addition and subtraction for Grade 2. Use friendly real-life situations like buying candies or sharing toys.

### Science
Quiz on the solar system for Grade 5 students. Fill-in-the-blank questions about planets, the sun, and the moon.

Teach about the parts of a plant (root, stem, leaf, flower, fruit) for Grade 3. Each question gives the function and asks which part it is.

States of matter quiz (solid, liquid, gas) for Grade 4. Show everyday examples and ask which state of matter it is.

### General Knowledge
Famous landmarks of the world for Grade 5 students. Show a description and let students pick the correct landmark.

Capital cities quiz for Grade 6 students covering 5 different continents.

Animals and their homes (e.g. bee → hive, lion → den) for Grade 2.

### Hindi
Teach Hindi vowels (अ, आ, इ, ई) to Grade 1 students. Show a Hindi word with one vowel missing and let students pick the correct vowel.

Quiz on Hindi opposites (विलोम शब्द) for Grade 4. Give a word, ask for its opposite.

## Story Mode

### Indian Mythology — Krishna
The story of Krishna and the Kaliya snake — kid-friendly retelling for 8-year-olds, end with the lesson that bravery comes from love not anger.

The story of how baby Krishna stole butter from his mother and the village — fun, mischievous, gentle tone.

Krishna lifting the Govardhan mountain to protect his village from rain.

### Indian Mythology — Ganesha
The story of how Lord Ganesha got his elephant head — kid-friendly retelling, gentle and warm, no scary scenes.

How Ganesha won the race around the world by walking around his parents instead — lesson about love and cleverness.

The story of Ganesha writing the Mahabharata with his broken tusk.

### Indian Mythology — Hanuman
The story of Hanuman bringing the Sanjeevani mountain to save Lakshman — gentle retelling for young children.

How baby Hanuman tried to eat the sun thinking it was a fruit.

Hanuman crossing the ocean to find Sita in Lanka — focus on his courage and devotion.

### Indian Mythology — Other
The story of Prahlad and Holika — kid-friendly retelling about faith and protection.

The story of Dhruva, the boy who became a star — about perseverance.

How Lord Shiva swallowed the poison to save the world during the churning of the ocean — gentle retelling.

### Panchatantra & Indian Folk Tales
The clever crow who dropped pebbles into a pitcher to drink water.

The monkey and the crocodile — a story about friendship and trust.

The lion and the rabbit — how the small rabbit outsmarted the big lion.

The four friends — a deer, crow, mouse, and tortoise who saved each other.

Birbal and the burnt food — clever Akbar-Birbal story for kids.

### Western Fables
The boy who cried wolf — about honesty and trust.

The hare and the tortoise — set in modern times, where the hare uses a scooter and the tortoise walks slowly. Lesson: patience and consistency win.

The ant and the grasshopper — about hard work and preparing for the future.

The three little pigs — about building strong foundations.

The fox and the grapes — about jealousy and giving up too easily.

### Original Stories for Kids
A little girl named Maya who befriends a star that fell into her garden — about kindness to strangers.

A brave squirrel who saves her forest from a storm — about courage and helping others.

A magical pencil that draws things that come to life — about responsibility and using gifts wisely.

Two best friends, a kite and the wind, who learn about freedom and togetherness.

A young elephant who is afraid of water and learns to swim with the help of friends.

A small firefly who thinks her light is too dim, but it guides a lost traveler home.

### Educational / Historical
The childhood of Mahatma Gandhi — lessons in truth and non-violence for young children.

How the first humans discovered fire — gentle, kid-friendly imagining.

The story of the Wright Brothers and the first airplane — about curiosity and never giving up.

How Newton discovered gravity — kid-friendly version with the falling apple.

The story of Helen Keller — overcoming difficulties and the power of teachers.

### Festival Stories
The story of Diwali — why we celebrate the festival of lights, told for young children.

The story of Holi — colors, friendship, and the legend of Prahlad.

The first Christmas — gentle retelling for young children.

The story of Eid — the lesson of sacrifice and sharing with others.

Raksha Bandhan — the bond between brothers and sisters, with a kid-friendly story.

### Animal & Nature Stories
A tiny seed's journey from the ground to becoming a giant tree — about patience and growth.

A baby bird learning to fly — about courage and trying again.

A little fish exploring the coral reef — about curiosity and wonder.

The seasons told as four sister friends who take turns visiting Earth.

A rainbow's birthday — how each color has a special gift.

## How to Get the Best Results

**Specify these in your prompt for a better story:**

- **Audience:** "for 8-year-olds", "for young children", "for teens"
- **Tone:** "gentle", "playful", "adventurous", "calming"
- **Constraints:** "no scary scenes", "kid-friendly retelling", "short and simple"
- **Moral:** "end with the lesson that..."
- **Setting:** "modern setting", "ancient India", "magical forest"

### Strong Prompt Template
```
[Story name or topic] — for [audience], with [tone/constraints],
ending with [moral if you have one]
```

### Example of a Strong Prompt
```
The story of Krishna and the Kaliya snake — kid-friendly retelling
for 8-year-olds, no scary scenes, end with the lesson that bravery
comes from love not anger.
```
